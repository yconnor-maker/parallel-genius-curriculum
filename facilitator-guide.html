Cohort Facilitator Guide

Everything you need to deliver the Parallel Genius curriculum as a live cohort experience. Timing guides, discussion prompts, and delivery formats for all 15 modules.

Delivery Formats

The curriculum supports three delivery formats. Choose based on your audience and time constraints.

Sprint (5 Days)

Full-time intensive · 6 hrs/day

Mon: Course 0 (all 5 modules). Tue: M01-M03. Wed: M04-M06. Thu: M07-M09. Fri: M10 + capstone project.

Cohort (5 Weeks)

1 session/week · 90 min each

Wk 1: C0 + M01. Wk 2: M02-M04. Wk 3: M05-M07. Wk 4: M08-M09. Wk 5: M10 + showcase.

Self-Paced

Individual · ~15 hours total

Students work through modules sequentially. Facilitator hosts office hours weekly. Async discussion board for peer support.

Session Plans

Prerequisite TrackCourse 0: AI Foundations

C0-M01 to C0-M05 (Bundled Session)

⏱ 2.5 hours total (30 min each) · or split across 2 sessions
Opening (10 min)

Ask: "How many of you have had an AI give you a confidently wrong answer?" Let hands go up. This sets the tone for the entire course — we're here to understand the tool, not worship it.

C0-M01: Confidence Audit Exercise (15 min)

Students ask AI a detailed question in their domain, then count the errors. Share findings with a partner. This is the "aha moment" for most participants.

C0-M02: Overload Inventory (10 min)

Individual exercise. Students list every AI-assisted project. The number always surprises them.

C0-M03: 10-Exchange Test (15 min)

Live exercise. Students pick a real problem and go 10 exchanges. Compare exchange 1 vs exchange 10.

C0-M04: Context File Draft (15 min)

Students begin drafting their PROJECT_CONTEXT.md. This is homework that carries through the rest of the course.

C0-M05: Build/Buy Discussion (10 min)

Group discussion: "What have you built that the market will replace in 6 months?"

What's the most expensive mistake you've made by trusting AI output without verifying it?
How many projects are you currently managing with AI? Does that number feel sustainable?
Course 0 is designed to be humbling. Participants who think they already know AI will discover blind spots. Don't rush the Confidence Audit — it's the most impactful exercise in the entire curriculum.
Track 1Foundations: M01 — M05

M01: The Dictation Method

⏱ 60 minutes
Teach (15 min)

Walk through the dictation pipeline: speak → transcribe → process → output → refine. Use the operative report analogy for clinical audiences.

Practice (20 min)

Students dictate a work problem out loud (or type stream-of-consciousness) for 3 minutes. Then feed it to AI and see what comes back.

Debrief (10 min)

Compare dictation output to typical prompt output. Which feels more like the student's authentic voice?

What feels uncomfortable about speaking unfinished thoughts to a machine?
Some students will resist the vulnerability of thinking out loud. Normalize it: "Surgeons have done this for decades. You're learning a clinical skill."

M02: Your AI Operating System

⏱ 45 minutes
Teach (15 min)

Present the Starter Kit concept: a standardized package of context files that any AI reads at session start.

Build (20 min)

Students expand their C0-M04 context file into a full Starter Kit: project context + brand voice + decision log + anti-patterns.

If you had to onboard a new employee in 5 minutes, what would you tell them? That's your Starter Kit.

M03: Writing for Machines

⏱ 45 minutes
Teach (15 min)

Task specification format: Context → Objective → Constraints → Output Format → Quality Criteria.

Practice (20 min)

Students rewrite a vague request ("make me a website") into a full task specification. Compare AI output from both.

What's the difference between "write me an article" and a task specification? How much time does the specification save downstream?

M04: Multi-Agent Orchestration

⏱ 60 minutes
Case Study (15 min)

Present "Two Agents, One Table." Walk through alphabetical partitioning as zero-infrastructure concurrency.

Case Study (10 min)

Present "The Sentinel's Stop." Discuss circuit breaker patterns and when agents should stop and ask.

Design Exercise (20 min)

Students sketch a multi-agent workflow for their own project. Where do agents collide? Where do they need circuit breakers?

If you had 3 AI agents working for you simultaneously, what would each one do? How would they avoid stepping on each other?
This module is the hardest for beginners. Use the case studies heavily — they make abstract concepts concrete.

M05: Visual-First Communication

⏱ 45 minutes
Teach (15 min)

Show the spectrum: raw data → table → chart → dashboard → scrollytelling essay. Each step up trades detail for impact.

Create (20 min)

Students take a data set or report from their work and ask AI to create a visual summary. Critique the output together.

When was the last time a spreadsheet changed someone's mind? When was the last time a visual did?
Track 2Architecture: M06 — M08

M06: Knowledge Extraction

⏱ 60 minutes
Case Study (15 min)

Present "Schema Drift" and "The Reconciliation Tax." Both demonstrate what happens when knowledge isn't properly extracted and stored.

Practice (25 min)

Students extract 3 reusable lessons from their most recent AI conversation. Format them as knowledge items with title, context, and application.

What decisions have you made in AI conversations that are now lost? How much did it cost to re-discover them?

M07: The Production Loop + M08: The AI Brain

⏱ 90 minutes (combined)
Teach M07 (20 min)

The production loop: Draft → QC → Revise → Deploy → Monitor. Show how each stage can be AI-assisted but human-verified.

Teach M08 (20 min)

The AI Brain: knowledge items, conversation logs, decision registries. Show how context compounds across sessions.

Architecture Exercise (30 min)

Students design their personal knowledge system: what tools, what format, what persistence layer. Present to the group.

Combine M07 and M08 — they're deeply linked. The production loop creates knowledge; the AI brain stores it.
Track 3Operations: M09 — M10

M09: Protecting the Human

⏱ 60 minutes
Read Aloud (10 min)

Read the "I worked and worked and worked" quote. Let it sit. Then ask: "How many of you feel this?"

Boundary Exercise (20 min)

Students define their personal AI boundaries: hours, scope, delegation limits, quality thresholds.

Case Study (15 min)

Present "The Decision That Vanished." Discuss persistence as a protection mechanism.

What is the most important decision you need to make right now that you've been avoiding by staying productive?
This is the most emotionally loaded module. Give space. Some participants will recognize themselves in the overload patterns.

M10: Durable Skills + Capstone

⏱ 90 minutes
Teach (20 min)

The 5 durable skills: domain expertise, editorial judgment, systems thinking, delegation architecture, quality verification. These are the skills that survive every AI upgrade.

Capstone Presentations (50 min)

Each student presents their personal AI system: their Starter Kit, their production loop, their knowledge architecture, and their boundaries. 5 minutes each.

Closing (15 min)

Return to the opening question from C0-M01: "What happens when you type something into an AI?" Compare their answer now to their answer from week 1.

What is the one thing you know now that you wish you'd known before this course?

Materials Checklist

For each session, ensure students have access to:

  • The module lesson page (online or printed)
  • The corresponding slide deck (for facilitator presentation)
  • The worksheet (printed or digital — students write during exercises)
  • Access to at least one AI tool (ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini)
  • Their Starter Kit file (begun in C0-M04, expanded throughout)

Facilitator-only resources: This guide + the slide decks (which include speaker notes visible only to the presenter).
