Protecting the Operator

Dr. Yamicia Connor, MD, PhD, MPH
The Labora Collective
01
The Real Bottleneck

The system is only as fast as the human can absorb.

You can run 6 agents in parallel. You can have 60 tasks in your pipeline. But if the human is overwhelmed — if YC can't review, decide, and approve — nothing ships. The human IS the bottleneck.

Every system design decision must protect the human's cognitive bandwidth.

02
From "Burnout Among Physicians"
"Burnout isn't a personal failure — it's a system design failure. When the system asks the operator to track more than they can hold, the operator doesn't get 'stronger.' They break. The system needs to change, not the person."— Dr. Yamicia Connor
03
System Design

How the AI Workflow Protects the Human

1

Auto-execute processing work

"Don't ask. Brain dump processing, dictation sweeps, dashboard updates — these are standing orders." (Lesson #25)

2

Visual-first formats

5 slides in 90 seconds vs. 3 paragraphs nobody reads (Lesson #24)

3

Audio review layer

Convert everything to audio so YC can review while commuting (Lesson #29)

4

One dashboard, not ten tabs

"YC looks at ONE place. Don't create separate docs she has to hunt for." (Lesson #9)

5

QC before human review

Inspector catches failures before they reach the human (Lesson #31)

04
Lesson #21
"The dashboard is NOT YC's dashboard — it's the Conductor's task tracker. YC is a human, not an AI tool. Her dashboard should show: her schedule, her concerns, decisions needed. The Conductor Task Tracker is a sub-view, not the homepage."— Conductor Lesson #21

This lesson was learned when the dashboard became a system monitoring tool instead of a human support tool. The fix: put the human's needs on the homepage, the system's needs underneath.

05
Physician Principle

The Triage Mindset

YC trained as a surgeon. Triage is the operating principle: assess severity, allocate resources, protect the critical path.

  • Critical: Needs human decision NOW — surface it in the dashboard
  • Important: Needs human review SOON — queue it with audio option
  • Routine: Can be auto-approved — handle without human attention
  • Processing: Standing orders — never ask, just do

The system must distinguish between these categories automatically. If everything is "urgent," nothing is.

06
Real Data

Context Switching Costs

YC's typical day

Clinical responsibilities + two companies + academic work + family. Context switching is not an inconvenience — it's the operating reality. The system must assume the human has 5 minutes of focused attention, not 50.

This is why every design decision defaults to:

  • 5 slides instead of 5 paragraphs
  • Audio instead of reading
  • One click instead of navigation
  • Auto-execute instead of asking permission
07
Anti-Pattern

The Decision Fatigue Trap

  • AI asks "should I do X?" when X is a standing order → wastes a decision
  • Multiple review queues in different locations → forces the human to hunt
  • Everything flagged as "needs review" → nothing gets prioritized
  • System generates more output than the human can absorb → creates guilt and backlog

A well-designed system produces LESS for the human to look at, not more. Filter before presenting.
