01
The Core Concept

A Large Language Model is a statistical prediction engine.

It predicts the next most likely word in a sequence, billions of times, very fast. That prediction engine — when scaled — produces behavior that looks like understanding.

It is not understanding. But the distinction matters less than you think — and more than you think — in different places.

02
Start here. Don't assume the audience knows this. Many people think they're talking to something that "thinks." Set the foundation: it's pattern matching at enormous scale.
The Four Things It Is

What You're Actually Talking To

1

A Pattern Matching Engine

It has absorbed the patterns of legal briefs, clinical notes, code, business plans — from millions of examples. It generates what "should come next."

2

A Context Window

It can only "see" a limited amount at once. Everything outside the window doesn't exist. Long conversations degrade because new content pushes out the beginning.

3

A Stateless System

Every conversation starts from zero. It does not remember your last session. Groundhog Day, every day.

4

A Reflection of Your Input

Quality out = quality in. Always.

03
Key Quote
"When you go to a brand new chat, a brand new tool, you realize how much of yourself is reflected in there. Because it just sounds — it's not helpful at all. It's just like, what is this thing I'm talking to?" — Dr. Yamicia Connor

The "empty chat" experience is the proof. Without your context, the AI is generic and useless. With your context, it's transformational. The difference is you.

04
Framework

Three Phases of Knowledge Work

1

Research & Brainstorming

AI is exceptional here. Thinking partner. Brainstorm, weigh pros/cons, test hypotheses. "Almost like having a conversation with yourself in computer form."

2

Structuring & Framework

AI excels at frameworks. "What AI is really good at is giving me frameworks that would be hard for me to develop fast enough."

3

Execution & Production

This is where the gap lives. The "70/30 gap" — AI gets you 70% there, the last 30% is yours.

05
Where It Fails

Four Critical Failure Modes

!

Clinical / Domain Judgment

It can write a medical article that looks sound. It cannot make clinical decisions. Only a domain expert catches the distinction.

!

Self-Awareness of Limitations

"The technology will not announce that it is operating from an impoverished conceptual base." It generates confident text regardless of accuracy.

!

Persistence & Memory

Decisions evaporate. Context disappears. The AI that knew your project yesterday knows nothing today.

!

Genuine Innovation

"AI couldn't have given me this idea. It just doesn't exist." LLMs recombine — they don't invent.

06
Case Study

The PROM Error

AI generated a clinical article about premature rupture of membranes. It stated "12-24 hours can be safe" for expectant management.

The Problem

The standard of care is immediate induction within hours at term. "12-24 hours" is a nuance only a clinician would catch — the kind of subtle error that sounds correct to a layperson but could harm a patient.

Lesson: AI doesn't know what it doesn't know. Domain expertise is the verification layer.

07
This is the slide that lands hardest with clinical audiences. For non-clinical: substitute your own domain. Ask: "What's the PROM error in your field?"
Key Distinction

Prompting vs. Conversation

❌ Prompting

  • One-shot input → output
  • Generic results
  • No pushback or refinement
  • AI's voice, not yours
vs.

✅ Conversation

  • 30+ exchange dialogue
  • Context accumulates
  • Correction is calibration
  • Your voice stays in
"I don't use prompts. I never write a sentence and expect an output. My work is too complicated. I've always used these through conversation."— Dr. Yamicia Connor

Workshop

Try It Right Now

⏱ 5 Minutes

Exercise: The Confidence Audit

1

Pick your domain

Something you know deeply — your profession, your craft, your expertise.

2

Ask an AI about it

Ask a detailed question in your area of expertise. Read the answer carefully.

3

Count the errors

How many things sound confident but are wrong, subtly wrong, or misleadingly simplified?

This is what AI does in every domain — you just can't see it when you're not the expert.

10
Takeaway

The One Thing to Remember

"It's not like you don't work. It's not like you go on vacation and the AI is there generating articles. That's not how it is."— Dr. Yamicia Connor

AI is a tool. A powerful, fast, sometimes brilliant tool. But it requires you — your thinking, your judgment, your domain knowledge — to produce anything worth using.
