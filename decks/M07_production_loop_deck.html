From "The Execution Gap"

The Loop

The Production Loop

Content production is a continuous loop. It never finishes — it just keeps cycling.

Ingest
Get everything into Coda as draft rows. Whatever state it's in. If it's not in Coda, it doesn't exist.
STAGE 1
Complete
Push drafts through the completion workflow: anchor image, text, references, social derivatives, sensitivity review, HTML build.
STAGE 2
Distribute
HTML template → Netlify deploy. Selected articles → LinkedIn + Substack. Newsletter + social promotion.
STAGE 3
04
Ingest

Stage 1: Get It In The System

  • Voice notes → transcribed → Coda row
  • Dictation dumps → processed by Conductor → task or article draft
  • Half-finished ideas → still registered as a row with status "Seed"
  • Zoom recordings → Otter transcripts → relevant quotes pulled
  • Slack threads → extracted → routed to correct project

The principle: capture everything, curate later. Low barrier entry, high barrier exit.

05
Complete

Stage 2: The Completion Workflow

1

Anchor image

Every article needs a visual anchor (generated or sourced)

2

Actual text

Proofread, stylized, in YC's voice — not AI voice

3

References & citations

Every claim backed, every source attributed

4

Social derivatives

LinkedIn post, tweet thread, pull quotes — pre-built, not afterthoughts

5

Sensitivity review

Medical claims, identity content, controversial framing — double-checked

6

HTML build

Branded template, responsive, Netlify-ready

06
Distribute

Stage 3: Ship It

Distribution is not a single step — it's a workflow of its own:

  • HTML → Netlify deploy via GitHub auto-deploy pipeline
  • Selected articles → LinkedIn with platform-specific formatting
  • Newsletter assembly — curated batch, not every single article
  • Social promotion — scheduled, not ad hoc

And then the loop restarts. The newsletter generates responses. Responses generate ideas. Ideas become new drafts.

07
The Bottleneck

Where Most Operations Die

"Most people have plenty of AI-assisted drafts. They have very few finished, published pieces. The bottleneck is never ideation. It's always completion."— From the Execution Gap analysis

The production loop exists to solve this. It ensures that every draft has a defined path to completion — and that path has checkpoints, not just intentions.
