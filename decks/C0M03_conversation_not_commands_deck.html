Conversation, Not Commands

The Difference Between a Search Engine and a Thinking Partner

Dr. Yamicia Connor, MD, PhD, MPH
The Labora Collective
01
The Problem

Most people use AI like a search engine.

Type a question. Get an answer. Close the tab. That's a search engine.

The power users who get 10× results? They have 30-minute conversations. They argue. They correct. They push back. They contradict themselves and then resolve the contradiction.

The difference is not the tool. It's the interaction pattern.

02
Key Quote
"I don't use prompts. I talk to the AI the way I talk to a colleague. I explain what I'm trying to do. I think out loud. I contradict myself. I explore tangents. I come back to the main thread."— Dr. Yamicia Connor
03
Key Distinction

Prompting vs. Conversation

❌ Prompting

  • Single input → single output
  • Polished, pre-constructed
  • Answer-seeking
  • Optimized for the machine
  • "Give me a business plan"
vs.

✅ Conversation

  • 30+ exchange dialogue
  • Messy, half-formed, honest
  • Understanding-seeking
  • Optimized for human thinking
  • "I'm trying to figure out whether..."

Emphasize: the dictation method doesn't require a recording device. Speaking to the AI chat box counts. The key is thinking out loud vs. writing polished prompts.
Why It Works

Three Reasons Conversation Beats Prompts

1

Context Accumulation

In a 30-exchange conversation, the AI has your goals, constraints, preferences, and corrections. In a prompt, it has one sentence.

2

Error Correction is Calibration

When you tell the AI "no, that's wrong because..." it recalibrates. Each correction makes the next output better. Prompts don't have this loop.

3

Your Voice Stays In

After 20 exchanges where you push back on generic language, the AI learns your register. It starts sounding like you — not like a template.

06
The Barrier

Vulnerability is the feature, not the bug.

"The idea of speaking your unfinished thoughts into a system and having them organized, challenged, and refined feels alien. It feels vulnerable. It feels like you're exposing incomplete thinking."

But that's exactly the point.

AI is most useful when you give it your incomplete thinking and let it help you complete it. Not your polished prompt. Your messy, contradictory, half-formed ideas.

07
Evidence

The Proof of Concept

"This article was dictated while managing three children, preparing dinner, and navigating an interruption involving hot milk."

That's not a humble brag. That's an engineering specification.

If the method works while managing chaos, it works. If it requires quiet focus and an hour of prep time, it's a tool for people who already have time to think.

08
Workshop

Your First Real Conversation

⏱ 10 Minutes

Exercise: The 10-Exchange Test

1

Pick a real problem

Something messy and unresolved from your actual work.

2

Start talking — don't prompt

Explain what you're trying to do, why it's hard, what you've tried. Use "I'm thinking about..." not "Give me a..."

3

Go 10 exchanges minimum

Push back on generic answers. Correct it. Tell it what it got wrong. Ask it to go deeper.

4

Compare exchange 1 vs. exchange 10

Notice the difference in quality, specificity, and relevance.

Takeaway

The One Thing to Remember
