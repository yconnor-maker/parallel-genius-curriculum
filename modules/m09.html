Module 09

Protecting the Human

The Cognitive Load Nobody Warned You About

Track: ProductionDuration: 90 minFormat: Lecture + Workshop

Overview

Core Thesis

AI doesn't reduce work. It intensifies it. The cognitive load of carrying AI-accelerated output in your head — knowing what's drafted, what needs editing, what needs formatting, what needs scheduling — is a genuinely new form of exhaustion that nobody warned you about. Protecting the human in the loop is a design problem, not a willpower problem.

"UC Berkeley found that AI doesn't reduce work — it intensifies it. Workers using AI produce more, at higher quality, at faster pace, with broader scope. And they work longer hours."

Key Concepts

What You'll Learn

1. The AI Super Productivity Paradox

Asana calls it the paradox: the bottleneck has shifted from production to absorption. You can produce faster than you can consume, review, and approve.

2. Five Things YC Actually Learned

You have to bring the thinking. You have to build verification in. You have to teach it your voice. You have to accept wasted time. You have to redefine ownership.

3. The $31,700 Billing Backlog

The Doctor agent built the entire billing system. But it banked on a 5-minute human dictation that never happened. Building infrastructure without a human-input gate creates the illusion of progress.

4. Ask Probing Questions Before Building

"Probing questions are a really good way to get me to think about different things I might not think about." Dictation triggered by questions is her primary input mode. Build AFTER questioning.

Workshop Exercise

Track Your Cognitive Load

Track your cognitive load for one day of AI-assisted work. At the end of each hour, write down: (a) what you produced, (b) what is now waiting in your head for follow-up, (c) how exhausted you feel 1–10. Map the pattern. Where does the system need a release valve?
