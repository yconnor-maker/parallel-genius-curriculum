Module 10

Durable Skills vs. Workarounds

What Survives When the Tools Change

Track: ProductionDuration: 90 minFormat: Lecture + Workshop

Overview

Core Thesis

Most of what people call "AI skills" are workarounds that will be obsolete in six months. The durable skills — precision framing, knowledge architecture, orchestration, dictation — are the same skills that made people effective before AI. The technology changes; the thinking doesn't.

"Let me tell you what this technology actually is. It is a pool. A very large, very deep pool of human thinking — organized, compressed, made searchable and retrievable at speed. Every framework it knows, it knows because a human being built it."

Key Concepts

What You'll Learn

1. The Pool Metaphor

"The pool does not refresh itself. If the researchers who ask the questions that surface the mechanisms are defunded — the pool does not know this is happening. It cannot detect its own impoverishment."

2. Confidence Doesn't Decrease

"The technology will not announce that it is operating from an impoverished conceptual base. It will continue to answer with the same fluency, drawing on whatever is left."

3. Durable Skills

Dictation (M01) · Architecture design (M02) · Precision framing (M03) · Orchestration (M04) · Knowledge extraction (M06) · Cognitive load management (M09). These survive every tool change.

4. The Renaissance Professional

The person who can dictate, orchestrate agents, design knowledge systems, and maintain quality control — while being the domain expert. Not a new role. The old role, amplified by technology.
