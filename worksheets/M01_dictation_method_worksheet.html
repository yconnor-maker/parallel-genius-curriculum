Part C

Hands-On Exercise: Your First Dictation

🎙️ Dictation Exercise

⏱ 5 min recording + 10 min reflection
  • Pick a real work problem. Not a simple question — something messy and unresolved that you're currently stuck on.
  • Open a voice recorder on your phone (Voice Memos, Otter, or any app).
  • Talk about the problem for 5 minutes. Don't write. Don't outline first. Just talk. Think out loud. Let yourself contradict yourself, go on tangents, come back.
  • Transcribe it (using the app, Whisper, or manual transcription).
  • Feed the transcript to an AI. At the top, type: "Below is a 5-minute voice recording transcript where I'm thinking through a work problem. Tell me: (1) What am I actually trying to solve? (2) What assumptions am I making? (3) What did I avoid saying?"
What work problem did you choose?
What did the AI reflect back that surprised you? What did it catch that you hadn't articulated?
Compare: If you had written a prompt for this same problem (in 2–3 sentences, typed), what would you have said? How is that different from what you dictated?
Part D

The Vulnerability Factor

"The idea of speaking your unfinished thoughts into a system and having them organized, challenged, and refined feels alien. It feels vulnerable. It feels like you're exposing incomplete thinking. But that's exactly the point."

— Dr. Yamicia Connor

🤔 Reflection Questions

1. During the dictation exercise, what did you notice about your own comfort level? Was it easy or hard to speak incomplete thoughts out loud?
2. What would you NOT say to an AI that you might say to a trusted colleague? Why? What does that tell you about how you're using the tool?
3. If "AI quality is proportional to the quality of thinking you put in," what's currently limiting the quality of thinking you put in?

Reference

Module 1 — Key Concepts Handout

Concept Definition
Dictation Method Thinking out loud into a system and having your thinking reflected back in structured form. Originates from surgical operative reports.
Prompting (transactional) A one-shot input → output exchange. Optimized for the machine. Answer-seeking. Most AI advice is organized around this.
Three Phases Research & Design (AI excels) → Refinement (dictation matters most) → Execution (AI falls apart).
The Plaud Pipeline Dictate → Transcribe → Process → Tasks → Execute. No keyboard required.
AI Super Productivity Paradox AI accelerates output while organizational systems remain unchanged, creating more coordination work, not less. (Asana, 2026)
The Execution Gap AI gets you 70% of the way. The last 30% is where the real work lives — and there's now 10× more of it.
Vulnerability as Method AI is most useful when you give it your incomplete thinking. Polished prompts produce polished mediocrity.
