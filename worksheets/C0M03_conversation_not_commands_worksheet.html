Conversation, Not Commands

Module C0-03
Time: 40 minutes
Format: Individual practice
Prerequisite: C0-02
Part I

Your Current Pattern

Before learning the conversation method, document how you currently interact with AI:

How many messages do you typically send in one AI session? (1-3? 5-10? 20+?)
Do you usually type a question and accept the first answer, or do you push back and iterate?
When the AI gives you a wrong answer, what do you do? (Start over? Correct it? Give up?)

Part IV

The Vulnerability Barrier

What felt uncomfortable about speaking unfinished thoughts to the AI? Was it harder than typing polished prompts?
Did you discover anything in the conversation that you wouldn't have thought to put in a prompt? What was it?
Completion

Before Moving to C0-04

  • I completed the side-by-side test (prompt vs. conversation)
  • I tracked at least 3 correction → calibration cycles
  • I can explain why conversation produces better results than prompting
  • I understand the dictation method and how it connects to surgical training
  • I experienced the "vulnerability" of thinking out loud — and saw the payoff
